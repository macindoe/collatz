## Provenance and pre-check

Main-session question (2026-07-16), following the merge of 14.15.4: does the trivial direction of the diagonal compatibility locus (integrally realized ⟹ `y₂ = y₃ = y₀`, Theorem 14.15.3.6) have a provable converse? Pre-checked with fresh throwaway code (main session, 2026-07-16; script deliberately not provided):

- **Admissibility-class conjecture:** for a fixed left-word prefix of length `n` (letters at positions `−1,…,−n`, `M_n = Σ mᵢ`), the set of doors `z` admitting the letter-prescribed backward chain to depth `n` was, in **400 of 400** random words (`n ≤ 4`, letters ≤ 2, full period of odd `z` mod `3^{M_n}` scanned), **exactly one residue class mod `3^{M_n}`**, and that class was **exactly `B_n mod 3^{M_n}`** — the composed-affine offset of `14.14.8.2`/`14.15.3.3`, reduced in `Z₃` (denominators are powers of `2`, units mod `3`). 0 class-count failures, 0 `B_n` mismatches.
- **Liveness is derived, not assumed:** whenever a letter-prescribed predecessor exists, its `G`-image (the present door) is automatically live — `3,248` random cases, `0` failures. (Consistent with `14.14.3.2`(3): `G`-images are always live.)
- **The negative-diagonal instance (exact, verified):** the single-letter periodic word `((2,1))^∞` has composed-affine fixed point `y* = B/(1−A) = (5/8)/(−1/8) = −5` exactly; and indeed `stratum(−5) = (2,1)`, `G(−5) = −5` — the classical negative `T`-cycle `{−5, −7}` (`T(−5) = −7`, `T(−7) = −5`), read in door coordinates as a `G`-fixed point at return time `m = 2`. So this word's `y₂` and `y₃` coincide at a **negative** integer, and (assuming the converse below) it is *not* integrally realized by any positive live door — positivity is demonstrably the load-bearing archimedean condition, not a technicality.

So the mathematical target is true as pre-checked; your job is to prove it at house standard. Your verification code must be your own fresh implementation, per AGENTS.md.

## The framing mandate (read before item 1)

Proving the converse **completes the formulation** of `14.15.3`(c); it does not open the locus. The combined statement — *a bi-infinite word is integrally realized iff `y₂(W) = y₃(W) =` a single positive odd integer* — is an exact characterization of the diagonal locus **in terms of the word's own two adelic limits**, and deciding whether those limits coincide at a positive integer, for any given family of words, is exactly as hard as it was: it *is* the Bridge, under a sharper name. The honest closing sentence is that the Bridge's symbolic formulation is now an equivalence rather than a definition-plus-one-direction, and nothing about which words satisfy it has been learned, except the classical negative cycles reappearing as negative diagonal points. Any prose implying the locus has been "computed" or the Bridge narrowed is a register violation.

## Queue, in order

1. **The admissibility-class lemma.** New subsection **14.15.5** (14.15.1–14.15.4 are merged and closed; never renumber existing anchors). For a finite left word `V = ((m_{−1},r_{−1}),…,(m_{−n},r_{−n}))` with `M_n = Σᵢ m_{−i}`: the odd integers `z` admitting the letter-prescribed backward chain to depth `n` (each step existing per `14.15.4.1`'s criterion) form **exactly one residue class mod `3^{M_n}`, namely `B_n mod 3^{M_n}`** — where `A_n, B_n` are the composed-affine constants of the word read deepest-first (`14.14.8.2`), and `B_n ∈ Z₃` reduces mod `3^{M_n}` because its denominator is a power of `2`. Proof route (verify, don't transcribe): induction on `n`. Base: one letter's admissibility is `3^{m} | 2^{r} z + 1`, i.e. `z ≡ −2^{−r} (mod 3^{m})` — one class. Step: on the depth-`n` admissible class, the map from `z` to its depth-`n` deepest predecessor is affine with `z`-increment `2^{(…)} t` per `3^{M_n} t` increment of `z` (powers of `2` are units mod every `3^k`), so the next letter's one-class condition mod `3^{m_{−(n+1)}}` pulls back to one class mod `3^{M_{n+1}}`. The identification with `B_n` should fall out of the same induction via `14.14.8.2`'s composition law (`B_{n+1} = A_n β + B_n` in the appropriate order — mind the order convention: the wiki composes deepest-first; state yours explicitly and reconcile). Cross-check one sentence: this strengthens the synchronization corollary `14.14.8.3` from "chains that exist agree with `B_n`" to "existence itself is the congruence with `B_n`".

2. **The converse theorem.** Same subsection. If a bi-infinite word `W` has `y₂(W) = y₃(W) = y₀` under the diagonal embeddings, for a single **positive odd integer** `y₀`, then `W` is integrally realized by `y₀` (`14.15.3.5`). Proof route: *(forward half)* `y₂ = y₀` places `y₀` in every forward cylinder (`14.15.3.1`'s nesting), so `y₀`'s deterministic forward orbit realizes the right-infinite half. *(backward half)* `y₃ = y₀` gives `v₃(y₀ − B_n) ≥ M_n` for every `n` (`14.15.3.3`'s Cauchy estimate), so by item 1 the letter-prescribed chain behind `y₀` exists to every depth; by `14.15.4.1`'s uniqueness the depth-`(n+1)` chain truncates to the depth-`n` chain (the mechanism `14.15.4.5` already made explicit — cite it, don't re-derive), so they union into one infinite chain. *(liveness, derived)* every chain door including `y₀` itself is a `G`-image of a deeper one, hence live (`14.14.3.2`(3)) — no liveness hypothesis is needed anywhere. State the **combined characterization** as a corollary: `W` integrally realized ⟺ `y₂(W) = y₃(W) ∈` the positive odd integers ⟺ `H_{p,q}(W)` bounded (`14.15.4.5`) — three names, one locus.

3. **The negative-diagonal reconciliation.** Same subsection, flat. The word `((2,1))^∞`: compute `y* = −5` exactly (as in the pre-check), identify it as the classical negative `T`-cycle `{−5, −7}` in door coordinates, and record: its `y₂` and `y₃` coincide at a negative integer, so by item 2's characterization it is not integrally realized — every finite window of it *is* positively realized (`14.15.4.2`), so its positive realization height must escape: `H_{p,q} → ∞`. Verify this **one prescribed instance** numerically (bounded: `H_{n,n}` for `n` up to a small fixed bound, exhaustive search with an explicit cap and the shortfall case handled honestly) — this single named check is explicitly permitted and is not a growth study. One reconciliation sentence tying the negative cycles' exclusion-by-positivity to the classical picture (the `3x+1` negative cycles `{−1}, {−5,−7}, {−17,…}` are famously why sign matters; cite spine.md/cycles.md where the wiki states the positive-domain convention, or record its absence as a finding rather than inventing a citation).

4. **Accounting and closing status.** The Bridge (bridge.md §16) is exactly as open as before: the equivalence gives the locus its third and sharpest name — *the words whose two adelic limits meet at a positive integer* — and no method for deciding that condition on any word family. Periodic words: the characterization plus `14.14.8.4` reproduces the classical rational-cycle-candidate dichotomy (the fixed point is rational; it is a cycle iff it is a positive live integer — which is `12.1`'s own integrality condition under a new name); explicitly not a new exclusion lever.
