Block family decomposer

A Collatz orbit unpacked into reduced blocks. Each cell is one representative of a state (ω, d): the value, then its symbolic form ω·2i·3j − 1 — so ω stays visible in every cell, not just the state label. Each family ends at its even lid, then s halvings exit to the next family (s belongs to this state alone — it can't be recovered from the next one). Solid cells are on this orbit; faded cells are entries other orbits use. Companion to Reduced coordinates for the Collatz map, DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21273548.
