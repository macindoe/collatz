One anchor, three operations

Three mathematical operations on the bit string of one bulk anchor M(ω), ω = 4996160569905494617, at 217 bits — each a different lens than the frequency tests of anchors.md §17.7.1–3. In every panel: M(ω) against a matched fair coin, with structured positive controls (ω=3, all-ones; Thue–Morse) proving each lens can see order. The framing: M(ω) is computable, so not Martin-Löf random — these test whether its finite description leaves a detectable fingerprint, i.e. pseudo-randomness. Companion to DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21273548.

Lₙ = shortest LFSR generating the first n bits. Fair-coin null: Lₙ hugs the n/2 line. A hidden linear recurrence plateaus — ω=3 collapses to L=1.

3 · Partial-sum walk (law of the iterated logarithm)

Sₙ = Σ(2aₖ−1), the ±1 walk of the digits. Fair-coin null: stays inside the LIL envelope ±√(2n ln ln n) and Sₙ/√n → N(0,1). ω=3 ramps linearly to +131072 (off-scale, density drift); Thue–Morse stays pinned near 0 (bounded sums).
